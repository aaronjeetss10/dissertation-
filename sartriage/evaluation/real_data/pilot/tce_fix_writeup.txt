Initial empirical evaluation on real-world aerial tracking data revealed a critical assumption flaw in the core priority triage logic: the system expected a kinematic transition (from moving to a sudden stop) to escalate an event to 'COLLAPSED'. However, real-world drone footage frequently begins recording tracks of individuals who are already stationary or lying on the ground, causing the state machine to perpetually trap them in low-priority 'SUSTAINED_STILL' states. We addressed this by integrating an initial boundary-assessment phase that analyses the morphological aspect ratio (width/height > 1.2) and stationarity of the first 10 frames, successfully bootstrapping true 'CRITICAL_STATIC' states without requiring the prerequisite kinematic drop.
