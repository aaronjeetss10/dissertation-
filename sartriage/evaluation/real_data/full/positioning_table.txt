PART C: Contribution Positioning Paragraph
================================================================================

Published aerial action recognition methods report their results at favourable
resolutions where persons occupy 50–200 pixels — the best-performing methods
achieve up to 18.8% mAP for action detection (Barekatain et al., 2017) and
recent prompt-tuning approaches (ICPR 2024) claim further improvements, but
all require either full-resolution crops or ground-truth bounding boxes.
However, at operational SAR altitudes (45–120m AGL) where 96% of persons
in our Okutama test set measure below 50 pixels, pixel-based classifiers
such as MViTv2-S degrade to chance level (26.5%, κ ≈ 0). SARTriage's
trajectory-centric TMS-16 classifier, which operates on scale-normalised
centroid displacement features rather than pixel appearance, achieves 60.3%
4-class accuracy at these same sub-50px scales — a 2.3× improvement that
represents, to our knowledge, the first demonstration that action
classification remains viable at operationally relevant drone altitudes.
The resulting 11-component pipeline translates this classification advantage
into an NDCG@3 of 0.41 for casualty triage ranking, surfacing the most
critical persons within the operator's immediate attention window despite
the severe resolution constraint.


NOTE ON FAIRNESS
================================================================================

This comparison is necessarily asymmetric:

1. Published methods report mAP (detection+classification) while we report
   classification accuracy (given tracked persons). Our task is harder in
   one sense (we must classify from centroid trajectories) but easier in
   another (we assume detection has already occurred).

2. Published methods operate at much higher resolution (960×540 or 4K)
   while our operational regime is 1280×720 with persons typically 15-25px.

3. The Okutama "action detection" task has 12 classes while our SAR task
   maps to 4 classes. Our 60.3% on 4 classes is not comparable to 18.8%
   mAP on 12-class detection.

The positioning argument is NOT "we beat published methods" but rather
"published methods operate at scales that don't exist in operational SAR;
when you evaluate at real SAR scales, only trajectory methods work."
